Part 1: Sentiment Analysis

Description:

The emotion of reviews can help theater getting to the know consumer behavior, what they want, what they like and dislike about the theater environment and equipment. So, we hope to predict the emotion behind the reviews.

Method:
1. Label the Data:

We only use two variables that are ‘review’ (one review of each theater) and ‘rating’ (the rating combined with the review). We assume that if a consumer is satisfied with the theater, he or she will leave a positive review and a high rating score. And if a consumer is not satisfied with the theater, he or she will leave a negative review and a low rating. So, if ‘rating’ >=4.5, this review is positive, and if ‘rating’ <=3, we consider this review is negative. The number of all theaters is 23664, and the number of labeled theaters is 19022. Our analysis is based on the names of these 19022 reviews.

The total number of positive reviews：13900

The total number of negative reviews: 5122

The total number of negative reviews: 5122


2. Clean and Prepare the Data:

We processed reviews of theaters, eliminating the stop words, special symbols, and uniform case. Converted the collection of reviews to a matrix of token counts. Then we divided the training set and the test set by a ratio of 8:2.

3. Model Selected:

SVM, Naïve Bayes, Decision Tree, KNN, Random Forest, Logistic Regression

Results and Conclusion:

As can be seen from the figure below, logistic regression achieved the highest accuracy (0.8192) in the test set, followed by Random Forest (0.8121). But Naïve Bayes has low recall and high precision, which means it will be easy to classify a positive review as a negative review. KNN and Random Forest models both have higher recall and lower precision, which means it will be easy to classify a negative review as a positive review. Since the accuracy of 3 models are more than 80%, we can identify whether a review is positive or negative with relatively high accuracy.

Part 2: Reputation Prediction

Description:

The names of theaters are very important. A good business name not only can it attract consumers, but also may affect the consumer experience. For example, consumers may be more inclined to give high marks to a theater with a fancy name because they subconsciously think the theater is trustworthy. We hope to predict the reputation of the theater through its name.

Method:
1. Label the Data:

We only use two variables that are ‘name’ (the names of theaters) and ‘rating’ (the general rating of each theater). We assume that the better a theater's reputation, the higher its ratings. So, if ‘rating’ >=4.5, this theater has a good reputation, and if ‘rating’ <=3, we consider this theater has a bad reputation. The number of all theaters is 8438, and the number of labeled theaters is 5529. Our analysis is based on the names of these 5529 theaters.

The total number of popular theaters：4352

The total number of unpopular theaters: 1177

2. Clean and Prepare the Data:

We processed names of theaters, eliminating the stop words, special symbols, and uniform case. Converted the collection of names of theaters to a matrix of token counts. Then we divided the training set and the test set by a ratio of 8:2.

3. Model Selected:

SVM, Naïve Bayes, Decision Tree, KNN, Random Forest, Logistic Regression

Results and Conclusion:

As can be seen from the figure below, SVM achieved the highest accuracy (0.8517) in the test set, followed by Random Forest (0.8445). The performances of KNN, Decision Tree, logistic are also great. But Naïve Bayes has very low recall and high precision, which means it will be easy to classify a theater with a good reputation as a bad theater. Since the accuracy of 5 models are more than 80%, theaters' name did have a relationship with theaters' reputation. It could be that the art of naming influences consumers, or it could be that certain theater chains, such as AMC, will win more recognition.

Part 3: Popularity Prediction

Description:

A business name is often theater’s first impression with customers. A good business name or trademark can attract more consumers. We hope to predict the popularity of the theater through its name.

Method:
1. Label the Data:

We only use two variables that are ‘name’ (the names of theaters) and ‘review_count’ (the total numbers of reviews of each theater). We assume that the more popular a theater is, the more reviews it gets. So, if ‘review_count’ >=50, this theater is popular, and if ‘review_count’ <=10, we consider this theater to be unpopular. The number of all theaters is 8438, and the number of labeled theaters is 6193. Our analysis is based on the names of these 6,193 theaters.
