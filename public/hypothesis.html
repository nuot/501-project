Hypothesis Test 1

We want to further explore the relationship between people working load and their evaluation to local theaters. Based on the top 100 hard working city rank, all the ranked cities were grouped to three categories: Working extremely hard, Working very hard and Working hard.

From the Boxplot, it’s not hard to find that working hard cities’s theater rating is generally lower than the other two groups. But we can not make deeper conclusion about if there are any difference between working extremely hard cities’ theater rating and working very hard cities’. Therefore, for detail information, we applied T test to help us figure out.

H0: People living in working extremely hard cities have the same attitude towards local theater with people living in working very hard cities.
H1: People living in working extremely hard cities tend to be more satisfied with local theater compared to who living in working very hard cities.

Since the P Value equals 0.0102, which is less than significant level 0.05, we reject the null hypothesis. Thus, there is enough evidence to conclude that there is a significant difference between two group cities’ theaters’ rating.Taking the boxplot into consideration, we can reach out a very interesting conclusion, that people living in more heavy working load cities tends to give local theater a higher rate.

Hypothesis Test 2

From the analysis above, we learn that workload can influence theaters overall rating, but what makes this phenomenon happens? In order to solve this question, our team take state median average income, the state popula-tion, theater average review counts into consideration, because income and population might have a relationship with the population diversity and the ability to afford entertainment.

H0: Population, state’s median average income and review count together contribute to each state average theater rating.
H1: State average theater rating has nothing to do with the combination of population, state’s median average income and review count.

From the review counts and rating boxplot, we can see that less number reviewsinduce some extreme case like rating 1 or 5, and the more reviews the average the rating tends to be.

Besides, from the graph we can see, it’s obviously that with the number of reviews in-crease, the rating close to 3.5 and become more stable. Therefore, we consider review counts as an important variable in analyzing rating.
